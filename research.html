Mathematics

Research Bio

I am a PhD candidate in pure mathematics at Victoria University of Wellington, New Zealand, focusing on algebraic topics with a range of applications in functional analysis (supervised by Lisa Orloff Clark and Astrid an Huef).

These topics utilise an understanding of intrinsic order-theoretic objects in algebraic structures, for example the cosets in inverse semigroups and filters in categories. In my postgraduate research, I have applied these tools in studying groupoids associated to the canonical action of inverse semigroups on their spectra and in generalising the construction of path groupoids from higher-rank graphs to include non-finitely aligned graphs.

While extending and applying the results of my postgraduate research, I am using the above techniques in collaborative projects in abstract harmonic analysis and twisted Steinberg algebras (a purely algebraic analogue to C*-algebras modified with cohomological data).

Data Science

Research Bio

I undertake data science research with a focus on climate-related projects. In particular, I have published work applying machine learning to estimate greenhouse gas emissions inventories using proxy data.

Publications
